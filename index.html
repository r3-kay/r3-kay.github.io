Game Level Design

What the role entails:

When you play games, think about what keeps you wanting to play more. Is it the environment? The way the character interacts with the world around it? Think about what makes a game interesting and exciting to play. This is the job of a level designer.
The process begins by sketching ideas out and thinking about how to best approach the level in terms of how the player could act, and all the possibilties of this. The game needs to flow well, with a clear path mapped out but one that is not forcing the player through a narrow path that will inevitably become boring. They need to think about how to get the player to intuitively trigger the events that allows the game to progress, without making it too easy or too obscure.

A level designer should:

  • be able to come up with creative ways of adding missions and challenges to a game.
  • have a good understanding of gameplay mechanics and be able to see all possibilities in terms of what the player could do, even when acting unpredictably.
  • be able to use scripting languages and understand the basics of gameplay programming.
  • have a basic understanding of physics and be able to accurately predict how objects will move within the game when the laws of physics are applied.
  • have developed 2D and 3D design skills and be able to use software such as Maya to create 3D environments.
  • be able to communicate well with other areas of the team such as the artists and design team. They will most likely work alongside the gameplay designers, gameplay testers, as well as the artists and programmers.

So how do I become a level designer?

If you are at school or in college, taking the following courses could be useful in landing you a job as a level designer:

  • Art
  • Art and Design
  • Graphic Design
  • Computer Science
  • Mathematics
  • Physics

Another option is to apply for Level 3 qualifications in one of the following:
  • Games Animation and VFX
  • Graphics
  • Digital Games Design and Development
  • Computing in Art and Design
  • Entertainment Technology: Video Games Art and Design Production
  • Digital Media
  • Creative Media Production & Technology
  • IT:Programming


But what if I've already finished college?

If you've finished college in a different field, then don't fret, you can still develop the skills to break into the level design career.

There are many ways to approach becoming a level designer, but the best place to start would probably be to play games! Think about the types of games you would be interested in creating, and while playing these games, pay attention to the layout of each area and how you as the user interacts with the surrounding environment. Becoming conscious of these things means that you are more likely to get a better understanding of how the level design process works and everything that you need to keep in mind when creating a level.

If you have already finished college, even in a different field, you can always consider applying for a degree. Although it is useful to have some knowledge on the topic already, sometimes it is not entirely necessary to be able to get onto a university course. Many universities have connections with people within the games industry so this would be a perfect opportunity to meet as many people as possible and start to expand your network.



Networking is more of a social way of getting links within the industry. This means going to events as well as attending game jams, and trying to build relationships with the people that you meet. Often these people will know other people who know people who may be looking for someone to hire. Networking is great as it gives you the ability to use your social skills as well as your technical skills to try to build a name for yourself within the industry. Remember to bring your contact details and perhaps a link to your portfolio and try to keep in touch with the people you meet. Adding them on LinkedIn is a great way to do this and expand your network online as well.

Buildling a portfolio will be particularly useful as this is where you are able to showcase your work. For most job applications, this will be essential. It is the most optimal way of letting your employer know what you are capable of producing.

Some game publishers create software that allows you to create your own mods. Modding is a great way to practice your skills, as you can make changes to practically any aspect of the game. You can make mods to change how the game looks, but also how it plays. This means you could potentially create a complete overhaul of the game. As well as being a great way of practicing, you could also create an area within your portfolio where you can showcase these mods (although it's best to keep them separate from your main portfolio)
